Good questions. Let me give you straight answers on all three.

---

## 1. Look-ahead bias — is the model cheating?

**No, it's clean.** Here's why:

- The train/test split is **strictly chronological** — earliest 75% of dates train, latest 25% are holdout. A game dated Feb 26 can never leak into training a model tested on March 10.
- `load_team_stats()` and `load_last4_pbp_priors()` both read from `last4_{date_str}.json` — date-stamped snapshot files. Each game only sees the stats file from **that exact date**, not an aggregate of the whole season.
- The closing line is used only in **grading** (`side_hit()`), never as a feature. In a real scenario, the closing line is known pre-tip, so no issue there either.

The one honest caveat: the holdout window (Feb 26 → Mar 22) is **tournament season**, which has different dynamics than regular season. That slightly inflates baseline expected-total accuracy because late-season lines are tighter. Not cheating, but worth knowing.

---

## 2. Should you be chasing CLV/EV instead of accuracy?

**Yes. The attached file is right and so are you.**

Here's the reframe that matters for this project:

The "gap" our pregame model produces — the difference between our projected total and the closing total — **is essentially CLV**. When we predict 155 and the line closes at 143, we're claiming +12 CLV on the Over. If we're right directionally more than break-even (~52.4% at -110), that gap is working.

What the current evaluator already does is the correct frame:
- Not "how close was our prediction to actual" (MAE)
- But "when our number disagreed with the market, were we right?" (directional O/U win %)

The tiered FULL/HALF/PASS policy is already your CLV filter — **FULL bets are your high-CLV wagers**.

The 65% win rate on 40 FULL bets at gap≥10 from baseline is interesting. The honest problem: 40 bets is too small to be statistically confident. You'd want 200+ to trust it.

---

## 3. Two-model GO/NO-GO — honest assessment

**Architecturally sound, practically challenging right now.** Here's the breakdown:

| Model | What it does | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Prediction model** | Projects a total, compares to closing line, gives O/U signal + gap size | Built ✓ |
| **Line movement model** | Tracks open → close movement to detect sharp action vs public steam | Needs opening lines ✗ |

The GO/NO-GO logic would be:
- Prediction model: gap ≥ 10 on the OVER → **GO**
- Line movement model: line moved AWAY from the OVER (i.e., sharp action also sold the Over) → **NO GO, pass**
- Both confirm → **FULL bet**

The real blocker: **we only have closing lines**. We have no opening line data. To build the movement model we'd need:
- Opening totals (when books first post, usually Sunday night for next week's games)
- At least one mid-week snapshot

The Covers scraper could potentially be extended to capture an early-week snapshot, but that's a new data collection effort.

**Shorter-term substitute**: Use the closing spread/total as a proxy for market confidence. A game where the closing total is exactly a round number (145, 150) is often a heavily-traded line — the book has shaped it precisely. That's a crude movement signal but achievable with what we have.

---

4. **Don't add the movement model until you have real opening line data** — a fake proxy will mislead you
